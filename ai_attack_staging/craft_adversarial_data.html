Craft Adversarial Data

The Craft Adversarial Data technique, which falls under the AI Attack Staging tactic, involves modifying inputs to an AI model to trigger a specific malicious effect, such as misclassification or missed detections, often while remaining imperceptible to humans. Depending on their knowledge of and access to the target model, adversaries can develop these inputs using various methods, including white-box optimization, black-box optimization, black-box transfer, or manual modification. The ultimate goal of crafting these adversarial examples is to evade the AI model's intended function or erode its overall integrity, and attackers may test their approach to ensure it works before executing their primary attack.
